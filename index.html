About the Parish

St.Thomas church, Vadavathoor established in the year 1992, was promoted as afilial church with definite territory in 2001. The parish has a population of 300 practicing catholic families. The church is about 5 km east of Kottayam town near by the KK road. His Excellency, Mar Joseph Powathil(Archbishop Emeritus), then bishop of Changanassery inspired and promoted Rev. Fr.Joseph Madathil, the vicar of Lourdes church to identify a place for the growing spiritual needs of the people around Vadavathoor. This was materialised in the year 1992. A small house in the 77 cents plot was converted into a chappel and holy mass was celebrated every sunday in this chappel. Very Rev Fr. George Alenchery, present Major Archbishop of the Syro Malabar church, the then Prof. at vadavathoor apostolic seminary took keen interest in the flourishing growth of this church and organised a well motivated spiritual community here.

About St.Thomas

Thomas the Apostle (Thoma Shliha: Aramaic: ܬܐܘܡܐ ܫܠܝܚܐ‎; Malayalam: തോമാ ശ്ലീഹാ) was one of the Twelve Apostles of Jesus Christ, according to the New Testament. He is best known from the account in the Gospel of Saint John, where he questioned Jesus' resurrection when first told of it, followed by his confession of faith as both "My Lord and my God" on seeing Jesus' wounded body. Traditionally, he is said to have traveled outside the Roman Empire to preach the Gospel, traveling as far as India. According to tradition, the Apostle reached Muziris, India in 52 AD and baptized several people who are today known as Saint Thomas Christians or Nasranis. He is often regarded as the Patron Saint of India, and the name Thomas remains quite popular among Saint Thomas Christians of India.

Message from the Vicar

"Go from your country, your people and your father’s household to the land I will show you. I will make you into a great nation, and I will bless you; I will make your name great, and you will be a blessing"(Genesis 12:1-2) It was the plan of God to bring us together and form a community in Vadavathoor. The astonishing growth our community have shown over these years asserts this fact. For a parish like ours, where many of the parishers are out of our state/country, this website will create a platform to come together and get updated about our community. I hope our people will use this website regularly and will benefit their spiritual growth and the progress of the parish.
With prayers & wishes,
Fr. Jison Paul Vengassery
Vicar, St. Thomas Church, Vadavathoor
